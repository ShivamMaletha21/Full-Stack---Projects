Title:How to Start Your Fronted Journey in 2026

Author:Shivam Maletha

Date:March 20, 2026

Content:
Starting a frontend development journey can feel overwhelming, especially with so many tools and technologies available today. However, the key is to begin with strong fundamentals. Focus on learning HTML, CSS, and JavaScript before jumping into frameworks.
Understanding how the web works, including the structure of web pages and how browsers render content, will give you a strong advantage. Instead of rushing into complex projects, start with simple layouts and gradually move toward building real-world applications.
Consistency is more important than intensity. Even dedicating one hour daily can help you build solid skills over time. Practice regularly, build projects, and analyze real websites to understand how they are structured.

TItle:Why Semantic HTML is Important for Modern Websites

Author:Shivam Maletha

Date:March 18, 2026

ContentSemantic HTML is more than just writing tags—it’s about giving meaning to your content. Using proper elements like header, main, article, and footer helps browsers and search engines understand your website better.
It improves accessibility, making your site usable for people with disabilities who rely on screen readers. It also enhances SEO, helping your content rank better in search engines.
Writing semantic HTML makes your code cleaner, easier to maintain, and more professional. It’s a small habit that makes a big difference in real-world development.

Title:Top 5 Mistakes Beginners Make in Web Development

Author:Shivam Maletha

Date:March 15, 2026

Content One of the most common mistakes beginners make is focusing too much on tools instead of fundamentals. Learning frameworks without understanding basic concepts leads to confusion later.
Another mistake is not building projects. Watching tutorials is helpful, but real learning happens when you apply concepts. Many beginners also ignore code structure and readability, which becomes a problem in larger projects.
Lastly, inconsistency is a major issue. Progress in development comes from regular practice and continuous improvement
