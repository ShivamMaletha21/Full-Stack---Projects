Environmental Pollution

Environmental pollution is one of the most pressing challenges facing humanity today. As industrialization, urbanization, and population growth continue to accelerate, the natural balance of our planet is being disrupted at an alarming rate. Pollution not only degrades the environment but also poses serious risks to human health, wildlife, and future generations.
What is Environmental Pollution? Environmental pollution refers to the contamination of the natural environment by harmful substances. These pollutants can be physical, chemical, or biological agents that negatively affect air, water, and land. The major types of pollution include air pollution, water pollution, soil pollution, and noise pollution. Causes of Environmental Pollution
Several human activities are the primary drivers of pollution: Industrial Emissions
– Factories release toxic gases and chemicals into the air and water.
Vehicle Exhaust –
The growing number of vehicles contributes significantly to air pollution.
Deforestation –
Cutting down trees reduces the Earth’s ability to absorb carbon dioxide.
Improper Waste Disposal –
Plastic waste and chemicals contaminate soil and water bodies. Agricultural Activities –
Excessive use of fertilizers and pesticides harms the ecosystem.
